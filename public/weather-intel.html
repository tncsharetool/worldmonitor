📐 How We Measure Anomalies

We compare current conditions against a 30-day ERA5 reanalysis baseline:

  • Extreme — Temp >5°C above / Precip >80mm above baseline
  • Moderate — Temp >3°C / Precip >40mm above baseline
  • Normal — Within expected seasonal range

Source: Open-Meteo ERA5 · NASA FIRMS · GDACS

What Climate Anomalies Mean for Travelers

🌡️ Extreme Heat Zones

When a region exceeds 5°C above its seasonal baseline, outdoor activities become significantly riskier. Travelers should plan indoor alternatives, stay hydrated, and consider flexible bookings. Looking for a cooler destination? Browse alternatives on Booking.com →

🌧️ Flood & Heavy Rain Warnings

Precipitation anomalies above 80mm/day signal flash flood risk, infrastructure disruption, and flight delays. Travel insurance that covers natural disasters is essential. Compare World Nomads plans →

❄️ Cold Snaps & Blizzards
